# Launch

## Background

Launch is moving from closed testing to production on Google Play. It's not the finish line — it's where the real work starts.

---

## Do this

### Before launch

- [ ] 100+ testers in closed testing
- [ ] Major bugs fixed
- [ ] Store listing ready
- [ ] Screenshots polished
- [ ] Privacy policy URL live
- [ ] Link tracking set up (Dub.co or similar — one link per channel)
- [ ] Welcome DM ready for new followers

### Store listing

**App name:** Short, includes keyword, no jargon.

**Description:**
```
Line 1: What it does (hook)
Line 2-3: Key features
Line 4: CTA
```

**Screenshots:** Show the app in use with real data. Add text overlays. First screenshot = most important. Never show empty states — the difference between filled and empty screenshots is massive (2x conversion difference).

### ASO (App Store Optimization)

| Element | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| App name | High |
| Screenshots | High |
| Description | Medium |
| Reviews | High |

### Launch day

- [ ] Submit for review
- [ ] Prepare social posts (Instagram + Reddit + niche communities)
- [ ] Tell your testers
- [ ] Post in niche communities (subreddits where your users hang out)
- [ ] Send DM to all existing Instagram followers
- [ ] Monitor crash reports

### After launch

- [ ] Respond to reviews
- [ ] Fix critical bugs fast
- [ ] Plan next update
- [ ] Track retention (not just installs — track uninstalls too)

---

## What to expect

Launch day will feel amazing. Day 3 will feel terrible. This is normal.

| Period | What happens |
|--------|-------------|
| Day 1-2 | Spike from your push. Best numbers you'll see for a while. |
| Day 3-7 | Sharp drop. Testers leave, curiosity installs churn out. |
| Week 2 | Baseline emerges. This is your real organic rate. |
| Week 3+ | Growth depends on ongoing marketing effort. |

The spike is not your growth rate. The baseline is. See [launch-spike](../07-growth/launch-spike.md) for more on this pattern.

### The tester drop

When you go from closed testing to production, expect to lose a chunk of active devices. Testers installed to help you test — not because they needed the app. When testing ends, they leave. This is expected, not churn.

Don't panic. Wait 2 weeks, then measure your real user base.

---

## Evidence

We shipped multiple apps from closed testing to production with 100-140 testers each. All approved without issues. The key was having enough testers and fixing the obvious bugs before submitting.

The best-performing launch got 80%+ store conversion and hit the 100+ install badge within 4 weeks. The difference? Quality screenshots, a clear value proposition, and a DM campaign to every Instagram follower on launch day.
