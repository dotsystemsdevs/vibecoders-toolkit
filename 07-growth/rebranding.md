# Rebranding Won't Save You

If your app is losing users, changing the name won't fix it.

## When rebranding makes sense

- You're getting trademark complaints
- The name is confusing or misleading
- You're pivoting the entire product

## When rebranding is a distraction

- Users are uninstalling and you think a new name will help
- You're bored of the current name
- You want to "refresh" the brand

## What actually happens

We tracked an app that was rebranded mid-decline. The data:

- Users were dropping **before** the rebrand (steady decline for weeks)
- The rebrand had **zero effect** on the decline — it continued at the same rate
- New name, new icon, same uninstall trend

The problem was never the name. The problem was retention — users tried the app, didn't find enough value, and left.

## What to do instead

If users are leaving, don't rebrand. Diagnose:

| Signal | Likely problem | Fix |
|--------|---------------|-----|
| High installs, high uninstalls | App doesn't deliver on the promise | Improve first-session experience |
| Low installs, low uninstalls | Nobody finds you | Work on discovery (ASO, Reddit, communities) |
| Installs stopped completely | Store listing isn't converting | Fix screenshots, description, social proof |
| Slow steady decline | No retention hooks | Add push notifications, improve core loop |

## The freeze option

Sometimes the answer is to stop. If an app has:
- Negative net user growth (more uninstalls than installs)
- Low store conversion (under 20%)
- No clear path to product-market fit

Then freeze development. Stop spending time on it. Keep it in the store (it costs nothing), but redirect your energy to products that are growing.

Not every app needs to be saved. Some were learning experiences.

**Takeaway:** Rebranding is a cosmetic fix for a structural problem. If users are leaving, the name isn't the issue — the product is.
