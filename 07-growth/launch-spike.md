# The Launch Spike

Every app has one. Here's what it looks like, why it happens, and what to do about it.

## The pattern

```
Installs
  |
  |   *
  |  * *
  | *   *
  |*     * * *
  |           * * * * * * * *  <- baseline
  +---------------------------
    Launch   Week 1    Week 2+
```

Day 1-2 spikes because you push every channel at once: Instagram, Reddit, DMs, friends. Then it drops to baseline — the organic rate your app pulls on its own.

## What the spike is made of

| Source | Sticks? | Why |
|--------|---------|-----|
| Your DM campaign | Some | Personal, high intent — best channel |
| Instagram followers | Rarely | Awareness only, most don't install |
| Reddit post | Some | If it resonates, real users find you |
| Friends and family | No | Support install, not real demand |
| Testers upgrading | No | Already had the app, just migrating |

## The baseline is what matters

The spike is vanity. The baseline tells you if your app has product-market fit.

**Healthy baseline:** 1-3 organic installs/day from store search or referrals
**Unhealthy baseline:** 0 installs/day after week 2

If your baseline is zero, the problem is discovery (nobody finds your app) or conversion (they find it but don't install). Both are fixable — see [no-organic-traffic](no-organic-traffic.md) and [low-conversion](low-conversion.md).

## Don't do this

- Don't measure success by the spike
- Don't panic when installs drop after day 3
- Don't run another campaign to recreate the spike — you'll just get another spike-and-drop
- Don't compare day-1 numbers to week-3 numbers

## Do this

- Wait 2 weeks before judging performance
- Track daily installs AND daily uninstalls — net growth is what counts
- Identify which launch channel brought users who actually stayed
- Double down on the channel that produced retained users
- Set a realistic weekly target based on baseline, not spike

## The real timeline

| Period | What happens |
|--------|-------------|
| Day 1-3 | Spike from launch push. Feels amazing. |
| Day 4-7 | Sharp drop. Feels terrible. |
| Week 2 | Baseline emerges. This is your real number. |
| Week 3-4 | Growth depends entirely on ongoing effort. |
| Month 2+ | Compound effect from reviews, ASO, word-of-mouth. |

**Takeaway:** The spike isn't growth. The baseline is growth. Everything after launch is about slowly raising the baseline.
