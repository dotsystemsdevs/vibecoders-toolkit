# Low DAU?

## Quick fixes

| Action | Result | Verdict |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Push notifications (staggered: 8h → 24h → 72h → 7d) | First uptick after weeks of decline | **Ship before launch** |
| In-app review prompt (after N sessions) | Keeps users returning + generates reviews | **Ship before launch** |
| Streaks / daily engagement hooks | Dropped — punishes users of event-driven apps | **Only for daily-use apps** |

## The testing-to-production drop

When you go from closed testing to production, active devices **will drop**. This is normal.

Testers install your app to help you test, not because they need it. When testing ends, they leave. That's not churn — that's expected behavior.

**What it looks like:**
- Day 1-3: Spike from launch push
- Day 4-7: Drop as testers and curiosity installs leave
- Day 8+: Baseline stabilizes — this is your real user base

**What to do:**
- Don't panic at the drop. Measure baseline after 2 weeks, not day 1.
- If baseline holds or grows slowly, you have product-market fit.
- If baseline keeps declining week over week, the problem is retention, not marketing.

## Tone matters

Don't guilt-trip users into opening your app. Passive-aggressive notifications ("You haven't opened the app in 3 days!") drive uninstalls.

Instead, be warm and low-pressure:
- "Whenever you're ready"
- "No rush — your data is here when you need it"
- "Quick reminder, no pressure"

**Takeaway:** Not every app is a daily-use app. If users come when they have something to do (log a round, enter a transaction), notifications remind — streaks punish. And the post-launch dip is your testers leaving, not your users.
