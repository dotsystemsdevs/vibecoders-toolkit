# Need Reviews Fast?

## Why reviews matter

| Reviews | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| 0 | Nobody downloads. App looks abandoned. |
| 5-10 | Social proof kicks in. Conversion jumps. |
| 10-20 | Helps ranking. More search visibility. |
| 50+ | Diminishing returns per review. |

Going from 0 to 5 is the highest-ROI move you'll make.

## How to get them

| Method | Effort | Speed | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|
| DM swap on r/googleplayconsole | Low | Fast | People post review swap threads. DM everyone who commented. |
| DM swap on r/AndroidClosedTesting | Low | Fast | Same community, many have production apps too. |
| Email test groups (Google Groups) | Low | Medium | They already have the app installed. Just ask. |
| DM past Reddit testers | Low | Medium | People who tested your app during closed testing. |
| In-app review prompt (API) | Medium | Slow | Google Play In-App Review API. Trigger after meaningful moment. |
| Post in r/androidapps, r/SideProject | Medium | Slow | Share your story, not "rate my app". |

## What worked

Went from 0 to 10+ reviews in one morning using DM swaps.

| Channel | Conversion |
|---------|------------|
| DM swaps (Reddit) | High — people actively want to trade |
| Email test groups | Low — ~1 review per 100+ testers |

**Best channel:** DM swaps on Reddit.

> Hey, saw you in a review swap thread. I've got an app on Google Play, would appreciate a review and I'll do the same for yours right away with screenshots. [link] Send me your link and I'll get on it immediately.

**Worst ROI:** Emailing test groups. Hundreds of testers, almost no reviews.

## The review campaign trap

Getting reviews is great. But don't confuse review installs with real users.

When you run a review campaign (DM swaps, friend asks, dev communities), people install your app to leave a review. Half of them uninstall within 24 hours. That's normal — they were never your target user.

**What this means:**
- Your install count spikes, but so does your uninstall count
- Net user growth from a review campaign is close to zero
- The reviews themselves are valuable (social proof, ranking) — the installs are not

**Don't do this:**
- Count review-campaign installs as "growth"
- Panic when you see uninstalls spike after a campaign
- Run review campaigns repeatedly expecting user growth

**Do this:**
- Run one campaign early to get 5-10 reviews
- Then let in-app review prompts handle the rest
- Track review installs separately from organic installs

## Watch out for

- Google filters suspicious reviews (new accounts, rapid swaps from same IP)
- Don't ask for 5 stars explicitly in the Play Store listing
- Reviews from the same device/account don't stack
- In-app review API has a quota — Google limits how often the prompt shows
