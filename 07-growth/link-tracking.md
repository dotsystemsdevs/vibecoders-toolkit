# Link Tracking

You can't improve what you don't measure. Track every link you share, and you'll know exactly which channels work.

## Setup

Use [Dub.co](https://dub.co) (free tier is enough). Create a separate short link for every placement:

| Link name | Where it goes | Where you put it |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| `appname-bio-link` | Play Store / App Store | Instagram bio |
| `appname-dm-link` | Play Store / App Store | Welcome DMs |
| `appname-tree-link` | Play Store / App Store | Linktree / link-in-bio page |
| `appname-story-link` | Play Store / App Store | Instagram stories |
| `appname-reddit` | Play Store / App Store | Reddit posts |

**Why separate links:** One link per placement = you know exactly where clicks come from. "Bio link got 15 clicks, DM link got 22" is actionable. "App link got 37 clicks" is not.

## What we learned

After tracking all links across multiple apps for a month:

| Link type | Clicks/link | Effort | Verdict |
|-----------|------------|--------|---------|
| **DM** | Highest | Active (send to each new follower) | **Best ROI per effort** |
| **Linktree** | High | Passive (set and forget) | **Best passive ROI** |
| **Bio** | Medium | Passive | **OK, not great** |
| **Story** | Low | Active (content creation) | **Not worth it** |

### Key findings

1. **DM links outperform everything.** Personal message + link = highest click rate. It feels like a recommendation, not an ad.

2. **Linktree/bio links are passive gold.** Zero ongoing effort, steady clicks. Just set them up once and forget.

3. **Story links are dead.** They disappear in 24 hours and get almost no clicks. Don't waste time creating story content for link clicks.

4. **Bio links underperform expectations.** People rarely scroll to your bio and click. DMs are 2-3x more effective per impression.

## How to analyze

Check your link dashboard weekly. Ask these questions:

- Which link type gets the most clicks?
- Which clicks actually convert to installs? (Compare Dub clicks vs store acquisitions)
- Are DM links growing or flattening?
- Which app has the best click-to-install ratio?

## Tag your links

If your link tool supports tags, tag every link by app. This lets you see total traffic per product, not just per link.

```
Tag: appname
  - appname-bio → 15 clicks
  - appname-dm → 22 clicks
  - appname-tree → 11 clicks
  Total: 48 clicks
```

## The funnel

```
IG follower → sees DM → clicks link → lands on store → installs → keeps app
    100%         80%         20%           20%            8%         4%
```

Most of the funnel leaks at "clicks link" and "installs." Focus on:
1. Making the DM compelling (so they click)
2. Making the store listing compelling (so they install)
3. Making the app compelling (so they keep it)

**Takeaway:** One tracked link per placement. Check weekly. Double down on what gets clicks. Kill what doesn't.
