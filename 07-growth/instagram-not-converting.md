# Instagram Not Converting?

## The hard truth

| Action | Result | Verdict |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Share-CTA reels ("send this to someone who...") | Best reach (80-94% non-followers) | **Good for growth** |
| Humor reels | Best engagement (loop rate, saves) | **Good for engagement** |
| Tips / educational content | Decent engagement | **OK for credibility** |
| Carousels | 0 engagement | **Don't bother** |
| Bio link to app store | 0 confirmed installs | **Nobody clicks through** |

**Takeaway:** 1K+ followers and 0 installs is normal. Instagram grows your brand, not your install base. Use it for awareness; use community posts (Reddit, Facebook groups) for acquisition.

## The platform mismatch problem

If your app is Android-only and your Instagram audience is mostly iPhone users, your store links are useless.

**How to spot it:**
- Link tracking shows clicks but zero installs
- Your niche has high iPhone adoption (fitness, golf, productivity)
- Followers engage with content but never convert

**How to fix it:**
- **Option A:** Ship on both platforms. Then every follower converts.
- **Option B:** Dual-platform DMs. Ask what device they're on, send the right link.
- **Option C:** If you have multiple apps across platforms, route users to the right one.

**Example dual-platform DM:**
```
Hey, thanks for the follow!

Are you on iPhone or Android?

iPhone -> [iOS app link]
Android -> [Android app link]

Both are free, no login needed.
```

This one change can fix a completely dead DM funnel.

## Why DMs work but bio links don't

| Channel | Clicks | Installs | Why |
|---------|--------|----------|-----|
| DM links | High | Some | Personal, 1-on-1, feels like a recommendation |
| Bio links | Low | Near zero | Generic, passive, nobody scrolls to bio |
| Linktree | Moderate | Some | Aggregates all links, captures curious visitors |
| Story links | Low | Near zero | Disappear in 24h, low intent |

**The pattern:** Active outreach (DMs) beats passive placement (bio, stories) every time. If you're not DMing new followers, you're leaving installs on the table.

## What to do

1. DM every new follower with a tracked link (use Dub.co or similar)
2. Add platform labels to bio links: "App Name (iOS)" / "App Name (Android)"
3. Track link types separately so you know which channel actually converts
4. Accept that Instagram is an awareness tool, not an acquisition tool
5. Invest time in community posts (Reddit, niche forums) for real installs
