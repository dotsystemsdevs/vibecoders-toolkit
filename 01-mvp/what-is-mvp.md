# What is MVP?

## Background

> "The version of a new product which allows a team to collect the maximum amount of validated learning about customers with the least effort." — Eric Ries

Until someone uses your app and comes back, you haven't validated anything.

---

## Do this

### Checklist — is your MVP done?

- [ ] One core feature works end-to-end
- [ ] You can demo it in 60 seconds
- [ ] A stranger can use it without help
- [ ] You're slightly embarrassed by it

> "If you're not embarrassed by the first version of your product, you've launched too late." — Reid Hoffman

---

## Evidence

### Famous MVPs

| Company | MVP | What they tested |
|---------|-----|------------------|
| Uber | SMS to call taxi | Would people order rides via phone? |
| Dropbox | Explainer video | Is there demand? |
| Amazon | Online bookstore | Will people buy books online? |

### What good MVPs look like

| Type | MVP feature | What you ship first |
|------|-------------|---------------------|
| Todo app | Task list with one twist | No settings, no themes, just the list |
| Tracker app | One counter on one screen | One screen, one button |
| Finance app | Input amount, see result | Minimal UI, core math only |

Ship in under a week. Everything else comes after real feedback.
